    • In the 1980’s at the age of 10 she was discovered as an artistic talent by Nevenka Žiger, curator in Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art, Rijeka. That was the beginning of her art education which was interrupted by the sudden death of her tutor in 1988. Ania stayed in art surroundings thanks to friends and colleagues from Rijeka’s artistic circles and kept the habit of participating in the local ex tempore exhibitions.

    Ania’s international career began in 2002 after a decision to live of her art work. After receiving numerous prestigious awards for her paintings, mostly in Italy, she found her second tutor Prof. Roberto Ascolese in Rome. Thanks to him, SKANI (the pseudonym coined from the combination of the first letters of her last and first name) had the opportunity to exhibit around Europe.
